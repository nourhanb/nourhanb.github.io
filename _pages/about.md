**Postdoc @ BC Cancer** — I build physician-in-the-loop AI for medical imaging and clinical decision-making (segmentation, classification, survival prediction) in CT, PET, SPECT, and ultrasound. I'm in the [Qurit](https://www.bccrc.ca/dept/io-programs/qurit/) Lab (Cure-It!) with [Arman Rahmim](https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=mE6_284AAAAJ&view_op=list_works&sortby=pubdate), and I founded **[Rising Scholars Hub](https://risingsh.github.io/website/)** (RiSH) to help students publish their first research paper.

<span class="bio-glance">PhD (UBC) · Vanier Scholar · Medical AI: lifelong learning, fairness, domain generalization · Teaching & industry: Cognia AI, Ankabut</span>

<div class="bio-more-wrap">
  <div class="bio-more-trigger" role="button" tabindex="0" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="bio-more-content" id="bio-more-trigger">▼ More about me</div>
  <div class="bio-more-content" id="bio-more-content" hidden markdown="1">
    I hold a PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of British Columbia (UBC), where I was part of the Biomedical Signal and Image Computing Lab ([BiSICL](https://bisicl.ece.ubc.ca/)) under Prof. [Rafeef Garbi](https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=Mwscz1IAAAAJ&view_op=list_works&sortby=pubdate). My PhD was supported by the **NSERC Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarship** and focused on improving AI for medical imaging: lifelong learning, domain generalization, and fairness and bias mitigation. I also collaborated with Prof. [Ghassan Hamarneh](https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=61DdlkAAAAAJ&view_op=list_works&sortby=pubdate) at SFU's Medical Image Analysis Lab ([MIAL](https://www.medicalimageanalysis.com/)).

    I have extensive experience in teaching, curriculum development, and lab management—as instructor, lab engineer, workshop lead, and TA—and in industry as an ML Engineer Intern at [Cognia AI](https://www.cognia.ca/) during my PhD and an HPC Expert Intern at [Ankabut](https://www.ankabut.ae/) as an undergrad.
